Desulfurization of Methanol Through Sonolysis

This past year, as my senior research project, I focused on development of a patent system for removing sulphur impurities from a hydrocarbon stream, which we are now applying to desulfurating waste methanol. You can find the original 2004 patent from Dr. Mahajan here. And you can find my working thesis document here.

The project is centered around sultrasonic sonication: a process that produces a phenonemon known as "acoustic cavitation." Effective cavtiation produces vapor filled vacuoles that rapidy form, implode, and reform, generating microscopic hot-spots of intense pressure and temperature, sufficient to strip Fe(CO)5 to iron-0, which will then react with sulphur complexes in solution. I also presented this work to the Chemistry senior research committee; you can find those slides and original artwork here.

Clathrate Hydrates

From 2019-2021 I worked within I-GIT on developing a wastewater treatment project, making use of the formation of propane-clathrate hydrate crystals to separate clean water from brewery sludge. propane hydrate "ice" forms at temperatures higher than water's native freezing point under moderate pressure, and allows for some unique processes. We've had many successes, and many failures, and this being my first real foray into academic research, I've learned a great deal along the way.

Here you can find a paper I wrote as an overview of clathrate hydrate nucleation, and some recent developments in the field. I also added some graphs from our own research for flavor. The paper was a good opportunity to absorb some knowledge from contemporary research and homebrew some more ideas for our own project. Literature review is an incredibly essential part of making informed decisions in the lab.

Printing Skin Organotypics

Through 2019 I contributed to an interdisciplinary project focused around the efficacy of bioprinting skin equivalents, as opposed to conventional pouring methods. The grafts were non-clinical, but we still practiced BSL-2 standards, and I learned a lot aboout biological systems that isn't otherwise covered in a chemistry curriculum. We printed collagen and gelatin scaffolds, tested the viability of keratinocytes post-printing, and formulated and fed our samples growth serum.

You can find the paper here.
